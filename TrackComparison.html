I-Box Track, Topper Track and Traditional Wooden Track Compared

There are many differences, while still having similarities between traditional wooden, I-Box, and Topper coaster models. First of all, I-Box, having its track fully steel is classified as a hybrid. It is steel track with wooden structure. Topper track on the other hand, is a steel box (grout filler) placed on top of a stack of wood. This makes it classified as a wooden coaster even though it has a feel like a steel hybrid. It feels like a steel hybrid for multiple reasons. First, the addition of a steel cross-tie on the Topper model, makes this coaster more stable than a traditional wooden ledger. Secondly, having the thin steel slabs (that the roller coasterâ€™s wheels run on) prefabricated makes this ride very smooth, something traditional wooden coasters do not generally have. Finally, Topper track having a steel box (grout filler) on top of the stack of wood, also makes it much smoother than having only a stack of wood like what the traditional wooden coasters have. This is why a Topper track RMC feels like a hybrid but is technically a wooden coaster.

Most of the time a steel coaster will be smoother than a wooden coaster. The I-Box track is almost always smoother than the other two. This is mainly because it is all steel. The Topper track is mainly steel but still has a wood stack. Since it is partially wood, it will naturally be not as smooth but with the rest of the track being steel and having lots of technology, the Topper model is still very smooth, just not as much as the I-Box. Finally, traditional wooden coasters are mostly rough. Unless they are prefabricated, they do not really have good technology from most of them being old and since generally, a full wood coaster is going to be rougher than a full steel coaster.
